Prosthetic Leg

A prosthetic leg is a limb used to replace or support a missing leg. Prosthetics can help people with leg loss move and perform daily activities more easily. Prosthetic technology continues to advance to improve the functionality and comfort of prosthetic users.

Tubular Chassis

A tubular chassis is a type of vehicle frame made of woven metal tubes connected to form the car's frame structure. Tubular chassis designs are commonly used in racing cars and off-road vehicles due to their strength, stiffness, and ability to withstand shocks and external forces. Advantages of a tubular chassis design include its light weight, high strength, and ease of modification. This design allows for easier adjustment to various vehicle types and terrain conditions.

Smart Safety Helmet

This helmet is primarily designed to improve worker safety in mining areas. It's equipped with an MQ-2 sensor that detects hazardous gases, which is then connected to a buzzer that receives signals from the MQ-2 sensor. The helmet also features a DHT11 sensor that measures temperature. All sensors are connected via a nodeMCU, which will eventually become an IoT-based system that can be viewed via a mobile phone.

Smart Safety Helmet

The development of innovative prosthetic implant technology to minimize bone loss after TKR or total knee replacement surgery. However, the pressure exerted by metal on the bone can cause erosion over time. This is increasingly important considering that the majority of patients undergoing TKR are people aged 60 and above. By providing additional cushioning in the form of polyethylene material in the gap between the bone attachment and the titanium metal material, it can reduce bone damage due to loads or forces and friction that occur due to knee joint movement.

DES (Drug-Eluting Stent)

Innovation in the development of DES (Drug Eluting Stent) technology to minimize dependence on antiplatelet drugs after coronary heart surgery. This technology is carried out by developing a DES design using PLGA (Poly Lactide-Co-Glycolide Acid) material by adding an aspirin layer. The drug coating process on the DES uses ultrasonic atomization, electrohydrodynamic jetting, and air-brush spray coating methods. PLGA material is a good biogradable material for use as a DES material and can perform diffusion absorption of aspirin which also meets FDA (Food and Drug Administration) requirements.
